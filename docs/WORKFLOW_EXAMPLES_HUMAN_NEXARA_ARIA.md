# NEXARA — Workflow Examples: Human, Platform & ARIA by Role

**Audience:** Non-technical (customers, CS, board)  
**Purpose:** Show how real people use NEXARA with ARIA in their daily work, in plain language.

---

## How to Read This Document

- **Human** = What the person does (clicks, decides, reviews).
- **NEXARA** = What the platform does (screens, lists, forms, limits, plan features).
- **ARIA** = What the AI co-pilot does (answers, suggestions, drafts, alerts).
- **How this helps** = Why this workflow matters for that role.

---

## 1. Owner / CEO

**Who they are:** Decision-maker. Needs a quick picture of what’s urgent and what’s at risk.

### Example workflow: Start of day

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA and goes to Dashboard. | Shows dashboard: campaign health, agent activity, escalation count. | When they open ARIA, it says: *"Ready. What needs your attention first?"* and offers: *"Show me what needs a decision"*, *"Which campaign is at risk?"*, *"Generate weekly brief"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Show me what needs a decision"** in ARIA. | — | ARIA looks at campaigns and escalations, then answers: *"3 items need your call: (1) CFO Vietnam — budget increase request, (2) APAC Brand — subject-line A/B test, (3) 1 escalation — Meta pacing alert. I can prep one-line recommendations for each."* |
| 3 | Asks ARIA: *"Which campaign is at risk?"* | — | ARIA replies: *"APAC Brand — reply rate down 18% this week. CPL still on target. Recommendation: approve the subject-line test and review audience next."* |
| 4 | Clicks the escalation in the dashboard. | Opens Escalations; shows the Meta pacing card with Approve / Reject. | — |
| 5 | Approves the recommended action. | Records the decision; removes the card from the queue; optional toast. | — |

**How this helps:** The owner spends minutes, not hours, to see what needs a decision and what’s at risk. ARIA turns data into short, role-relevant answers so they can act fast.

## 2. Founder

**Who they are:** Same high-level view as owner, but prefers a simpler menu and fewer clicks.

### Example workflow: Quick check and weekly brief

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Logs in; sees a compact sidebar (Dashboard, Campaigns, Revenue, ARIA, Settings). | Shows founder-style nav: only the main areas they use. | Same opening as owner: *"Ready. What needs your attention first?"* and the same quick actions. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Generate weekly brief"** in ARIA. | — | ARIA pulls performance and activity, then returns: *"This week: 94 leads (target 80). Best campaign: CFO Vietnam. One escalation (Meta pacing — approved). Next week: consider refreshing APAC audience. Full brief attached."* |
| 3 | Reads the brief; goes to Campaigns to skim the list. | Campaign list with status and health; no extra sections they don’t need. | — |

**How this helps:** The founder gets one place and one AI that answers “what matters?” and “what should I do?” without digging through every screen.

## 3. Strategic Advisor

**Who they are:** Focus on strategy, positioning, and client briefs — not day-to-day execution.

### Example workflow: Strategy review

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA; goes to Campaigns or Dashboard. | Full sidebar (research, campaigns, content, etc.). | ARIA says: *"I've reviewed all client briefs. 3 need your strategic input."* Quick actions: *"Review strategy items"*, *"Run competitive scan"*, *"Draft positioning memo"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Review strategy items"**. | — | ARIA lists the 3 briefs and what’s missing or weak: *"Brief A: ICP is broad — suggest narrowing to manufacturing 200+. Brief B: competitor section empty — I can run a scan. Brief C: ready for your sign-off."* |
| 3 | Asks: *"Run competitive scan for Brief B."* | — | ARIA runs a competitive scan (tool) and returns: *"Top 3 competitors for this space: X, Y, Z. Positioning gaps: [summary]. I can draft a one-page positioning memo."* |
| 4 | Says: *"Draft the positioning memo."* | — | ARIA creates a short memo; human can copy into the brief or attach. |
| 5 | Opens the campaign for Brief B and updates strategy. | Campaign detail → Strategy tab; form with brief, ICP, positioning. | — |

**How this helps:** The advisor stays at the strategy level. NEXARA keeps briefs and campaigns in one place; ARIA does the scanning and drafting so they can focus on judgment and client dialogue.

## 4. Client Success Manager (CSM)

**Who they are:** Manages specific clients (e.g. Medglobal). Needs call prep, reports, and check-ins.

### Example workflow: Before a client call

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Logs in; selects client “Medglobal” in the client switcher. | Top bar shows active client; sidebar shows CSM-relevant areas (Dashboard, Revenue, Content, Settings). | ARIA says: *"Medglobal call is tomorrow. Want me to prep your brief?"* Quick actions: *"Prepare call brief"*, *"Generate client report"*, *"Draft check-in email"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Prepare call brief"**. | — | ARIA pulls Medglobal’s campaigns, recent activity, and escalations, then answers: *"Medglobal call brief: 2 active campaigns. CFO Vietnam — on track, 12 demos. APAC Brand — reply rate down; we’ve recommended A/B test. No open escalations. Suggested talking points: [3 bullets]. Want a one-pager?"* |
| 3 | Asks: *"Generate client report for this month."* | — | ARIA returns a short report: leads, spend, top channels, one risk and one recommendation. |
| 4 | Clicks **"Draft check-in email"**. | — | ARIA drafts a friendly check-in email the CSM can edit and send. |
| 5 | Opens Content or Campaigns to confirm numbers. | Content library and campaign list filtered by Medglobal (or current client). | — |

**How this helps:** The CSM stops juggling spreadsheets and emails. NEXARA keeps client context in one workspace; ARIA turns that context into call briefs, reports, and drafts so they can be more present in the conversation.

## 5. Media Buyer

**Who they are:** Owns ad spend and performance. Needs to spot issues and optimize quickly.

### Example workflow: Optimize ad performance

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA; goes to Dashboard or Meta/Social. | Sidebar shows Ops, Revenue, Content, Social/Ads. No Inbox. | ARIA says: *"Meta is performing. One audience showing burnout — want to fix it?"* Quick actions: *"Optimize ad spend"*, *"Refresh audience"*, *"Pull performance report"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Optimize ad spend"**. | — | ARIA looks at spend and performance, then: *"Ad Set 3 has highest CPL and declining CTR. Recommendation: pause Ad Set 3 and shift 20% to Ad Set 1. Approve to apply."* |
| 3 | Asks: *"Pull performance report for this week."* | — | ARIA returns: spend, leads, CPL by set, top creatives, and one-sentence recommendation. |
| 4 | Approves the pause in the escalation or campaign view. | Escalations or campaign detail shows the recommended action; Approve applies the change. | — |
| 5 | Checks Social or Ads page for updated numbers. | Social/Ads page shows connected accounts and performance. | — |

**How this helps:** The media buyer gets ARIA to point at the problem and suggest a concrete move. NEXARA keeps ads, escalations, and approvals in one place so they can act without switching tools.

## 6. Content Strategist

### Example workflow: Content review and calendar

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA; goes to Content or Dashboard. | Sidebar: Content, ARIA Intelligence, Settings. | ARIA says: *"3 content pieces are waiting for your review."* Quick actions: *"Show pending reviews"*, *"Check brand consistency"*, *"Draft content calendar"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Show pending reviews"**. | — | ARIA lists the 3 pieces with IDs and status: *"CAMP-c1-email-1 (in_review), CAMP-c1-ad-2 (revision_requested), CAMP-c2-blog-1 (in_review). Open in Content library?"* |
| 3 | Asks: *"Check brand consistency for the last 5 emails."* | — | ARIA compares tone and messaging: *"4 of 5 align with brand guide. Email 3 uses a more casual sign-off — suggest tightening to match the rest."* |
| 4 | Clicks **"Draft content calendar"**. | — | ARIA suggests a simple calendar: themes by week, content types, and which campaign each supports. |
| 5 | Opens Content library and approves or requests revisions. | Content library with filters and approval actions. | — |

**How this helps:** The content strategist sees what’s waiting and gets ARIA to flag consistency and draft calendars. NEXARA keeps all content and approval states in one place.

## 7. SDR / Outreach

**Who they are:** Runs sequences and replies. Needs hot leads and ready-made replies.

### Example workflow: Hot replies and follow-ups

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA; goes to Inbox or Outreach. | Sidebar: Core, Ops, Campaigns, Settings. No Escalations. | ARIA says: *"You have 3 hot replies. Want me to draft responses?"* Quick actions: *"Draft replies for hot leads"*, *"Who should I contact next?"*, *"Write a follow-up for Karim"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Draft replies for hot leads"**. | — | ARIA lists the 3 prospects and drafts a short reply for each, using context from the thread and ICP. |
| 3 | Asks: *"Who should I contact next?"* | — | ARIA suggests: *"Next 5: Karim (opened 3x), Sarah (replied to subject A), … Priority: Karim — ready for a follow-up."* |
| 4 | Says: *"Write a follow-up for Karim."* | — | ARIA drafts a follow-up; human edits and sends from Inbox. |
| 5 | Sends the replies from Inbox. | Inbox shows threads; send or schedule per channel. | — |

**How this helps:** The SDR spends less time staring at a blank screen. ARIA drafts replies and suggests who to contact next; NEXARA keeps inbox and campaigns in one workspace.

## 8. Analyst

**Who they are:** Explains performance and builds reports. Read-only on escalations.

### Example workflow: Explain performance and build report

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Opens NEXARA; goes to Dashboard or Analytics. | Sidebar: Ops, Research, Revenue, etc. Escalations in read-only (view only, no Approve). | ARIA says: *"This week's data is ready. CPL improved 18% — want the breakdown?"* Quick actions: *"Explain what's driving performance"*, *"Generate weekly report"*, *"Find the top channel"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"Explain what's driving performance"**. | — | ARIA answers: *"CPL down 18% mainly from: (1) Ad Set 1 scaling, (2) subject-line test on Sequence A, (3) fewer low-intent leads from LinkedIn. I can add this to the weekly report."* |
| 3 | Asks: *"Generate weekly report."* | — | ARIA produces a one-page report: metrics, comparison to prior week, top drivers, one risk. |
| 4 | Asks: *"Find the top channel."* | — | ARIA returns: *"Meta: 94 leads, $196 CPL. LinkedIn: 28 leads, higher CPL but better conversion. Recommendation: keep Meta as primary; test LinkedIn creative."* |
| 5 | Copies or exports for stakeholders. | Analytics and dashboard show the same underlying data. | — |

**How this helps:** The analyst gets ARIA to do the first pass on “why” and “so what.” NEXARA gives one place for data and reports; they add judgment and present.

## 9. Client (external)

**Who they are:** Agency’s client. Sees a simplified view: their results and content.

### Example workflow: Check results and next steps

| Step | Human | NEXARA | ARIA |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | Logs into client portal (Agency plan). | Simple layout: Dashboard, Content, Inbox, Settings. No internal ops or team. | ARIA says: *"Hi! Your campaign is on track — 312 leads so far this month."* Quick actions: *"How are my results?"*, *"When will I hit my goal?"*, *"What happens next week?"*. |
| 2 | Clicks **"How are my results?"** | — | ARIA answers in plain language: *"This month: 312 leads (target 300). Best channel: Meta. 2 pieces in approval — we’ll publish next week. Anything specific you want to see?"* |
| 3 | Asks: *"When will I hit my goal?"* | — | ARIA: *"At current pace you’ll reach 320 leads by month end. To hit 350 we’d need a small boost on Meta or LinkedIn — your team can outline options."* |
| 4 | Opens Content to see what’s approved. | Content tab shows only what’s relevant to the client (e.g. approved or published). | — |

**How this helps:** The client gets answers without waiting for an email. NEXARA gives them one place to see results and content; ARIA speaks in simple, campaign-level language.

## Summary: How the Platform Helps (Non-Technical)

| Role | What NEXARA gives them | What ARIA does for them |
|------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Owner / Founder** | One dashboard and minimal clutter (founder: fewer menu items). | Answers “what needs a decision?” and “what’s at risk?”; generates a weekly brief. |
| **Advisor** | Full access to campaigns and strategy. | Reviews briefs, runs competitive scan, drafts positioning memos. |
| **CSM** | Client switcher and client-focused screens. | Prepares call briefs, client reports, and check-in emails for the active client. |
| **Media buyer** | Ads, performance, and escalations in one place. | Flags underperforming audiences, suggests optimizations, pulls reports. |
| **Content strategist** | Content library and approval flow. | Surfaces pending reviews, checks brand consistency, drafts content calendar. |
| **SDR** | Inbox and outreach in one workspace. | Drafts replies for hot leads, suggests who to contact next, writes follow-ups. |
| **Analyst** | Data and reports in one place; read-only escalations. | Explains what’s driving performance, generates reports, finds top channel. |
| **Client** | Simple portal with results and content. | Answers “how are my results?”, “when do I hit my goal?”, “what’s next?” in plain language. |

In every case, **the human decides**; **NEXARA** is the single place they work; **ARIA** is the assistant that answers questions and drafts next steps so they can focus on judgment and relationships.
