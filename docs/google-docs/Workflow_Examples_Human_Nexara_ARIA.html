NEXARA — Workflow Examples: Human, Platform & ARIA by Role

Audience: Non-technical (customers, CS, board)

Purpose: Show how real people use NEXARA with ARIA in their daily work, in plain language.

How to Read This Document

  • Human = What the person does (clicks, decides, reviews).
  • NEXARA = What the platform does (screens, lists, forms, limits, plan features).
  • ARIA = What the AI co-pilot does (answers, suggestions, drafts, alerts).
  • How this helps = Why this workflow matters for that role.

1. Owner / CEO

Who they are: Decision-maker. Needs a quick picture of what's urgent and what's at risk.

Example workflow: Start of day

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA and goes to Dashboard.Shows dashboard: campaign health, agent activity, escalation count.When they open ARIA, it says: "Ready. What needs your attention first?" and offers: "Show me what needs a decision", "Which campaign is at risk?", "Generate weekly brief".
2Clicks "Show me what needs a decision" in ARIA.—ARIA looks at campaigns and escalations, then answers: "3 items need your call: (1) CFO Vietnam — budget increase request, (2) APAC Brand — subject-line A/B test, (3) 1 escalation — Meta pacing alert. I can prep one-line recommendations for each."
3Asks ARIA: "Which campaign is at risk?"—ARIA replies: "APAC Brand — reply rate down 18% this week. CPL still on target. Recommendation: approve the subject-line test and review audience next."
4Clicks the escalation in the dashboard.Opens Escalations; shows the Meta pacing card with Approve / Reject.—
5Approves the recommended action.Records the decision; removes the card from the queue.—

How this helps: The owner spends minutes, not hours, to see what needs a decision and what's at risk. ARIA turns data into short, role-relevant answers so they can act fast.

2. Founder

Who they are: Same high-level view as owner, but prefers a simpler menu and fewer clicks.

Example workflow: Quick check and weekly brief

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Logs in; sees a compact sidebar (Dashboard, Campaigns, Revenue, ARIA, Settings).Shows founder-style nav: only the main areas they use.Same opening as owner: "Ready. What needs your attention first?" and the same quick actions.
2Clicks "Generate weekly brief" in ARIA.—ARIA pulls performance and activity, then returns: "This week: 94 leads (target 80). Best campaign: CFO Vietnam. One escalation (Meta pacing — approved). Next week: consider refreshing APAC audience. Full brief attached."
3Reads the brief; goes to Campaigns to skim the list.Campaign list with status and health.—

How this helps: The founder gets one place and one AI that answers "what matters?" and "what should I do?" without digging through every screen.

3. Strategic Advisor

Who they are: Focus on strategy, positioning, and client briefs — not day-to-day execution.

Example workflow: Strategy review

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA; goes to Campaigns or Dashboard.Full sidebar (research, campaigns, content, etc.).ARIA says: "I've reviewed all client briefs. 3 need your strategic input." Quick actions: "Review strategy items", "Run competitive scan", "Draft positioning memo".
2Clicks "Review strategy items".—ARIA lists the 3 briefs and what's missing or weak.
3Asks: "Run competitive scan for Brief B."—ARIA runs a competitive scan and returns top competitors and positioning gaps; offers to draft a one-page memo.
4Says: "Draft the positioning memo."—ARIA creates a short memo; human can copy into the brief or attach.
5Opens the campaign for Brief B and updates strategy.Campaign detail → Strategy tab; form with brief, ICP, positioning.—

How this helps: The advisor stays at the strategy level. NEXARA keeps briefs and campaigns in one place; ARIA does the scanning and drafting so they can focus on judgment and client dialogue.

4. Client Success Manager (CSM)

Who they are: Manages specific clients (e.g. Medglobal). Needs call prep, reports, and check-ins.

Example workflow: Before a client call

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Logs in; selects client "Medglobal" in the client switcher.Top bar shows active client; sidebar shows CSM-relevant areas.ARIA says: "Medglobal call is tomorrow. Want me to prep your brief?" Quick actions: "Prepare call brief", "Generate client report", "Draft check-in email".
2Clicks "Prepare call brief".—ARIA pulls Medglobal's campaigns, recent activity, escalations; returns talking points and optional one-pager.
3Asks: "Generate client report for this month."—ARIA returns a short report: leads, spend, top channels, one risk and one recommendation.
4Clicks "Draft check-in email".—ARIA drafts a friendly check-in email the CSM can edit and send.

How this helps: The CSM stops juggling spreadsheets and emails. NEXARA keeps client context in one workspace; ARIA turns that context into call briefs, reports, and drafts.

5. Media Buyer

Who they are: Owns ad spend and performance. Needs to spot issues and optimize quickly.

Example workflow: Optimize ad performance

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA; goes to Dashboard or Meta/Social.Sidebar shows Ops, Revenue, Content, Social/Ads. No Inbox.ARIA says: "Meta is performing. One audience showing burnout — want to fix it?" Quick actions: "Optimize ad spend", "Refresh audience", "Pull performance report".
2Clicks "Optimize ad spend".—ARIA identifies underperforming ad set; recommends pause and shift budget; asks for approval to apply.
3Approves the pause in the escalation or campaign view.Escalations or campaign detail shows the recommended action; Approve applies the change.—

How this helps: The media buyer gets ARIA to point at the problem and suggest a concrete move. NEXARA keeps ads, escalations, and approvals in one place.

6. Content Strategist

Who they are: Owns content quality, calendar, and brand. Needs pending reviews and consistency checks.

Example workflow: Content review and calendar

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA; goes to Content or Dashboard.Sidebar: Content, ARIA Intelligence, Settings.ARIA says: "3 content pieces are waiting for your review." Quick actions: "Show pending reviews", "Check brand consistency", "Draft content calendar".
2Clicks "Show pending reviews".—ARIA lists the 3 pieces with IDs and status; offers to open in Content library.
3Asks: "Check brand consistency for the last 5 emails."—ARIA compares tone and messaging; flags any that don't align with brand guide.
4Clicks "Draft content calendar".—ARIA suggests a simple calendar: themes by week, content types, and which campaign each supports.

How this helps: The content strategist sees what's waiting and gets ARIA to flag consistency and draft calendars. NEXARA keeps all content and approval states in one place.

7. SDR / Outreach

Who they are: Runs sequences and replies. Needs hot leads and ready-made replies.

Example workflow: Hot replies and follow-ups

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA; goes to Inbox or Outreach.Sidebar: Core, Ops, Campaigns, Settings. No Escalations.ARIA says: "You have 3 hot replies. Want me to draft responses?" Quick actions: "Draft replies for hot leads", "Who should I contact next?", "Write a follow-up for Karim".
2Clicks "Draft replies for hot leads".—ARIA lists the 3 prospects and drafts a short reply for each, using context from the thread and ICP.
3Asks: "Who should I contact next?"—ARIA suggests next 5 with priority (e.g. Karim — ready for follow-up).
4Says: "Write a follow-up for Karim."—ARIA drafts a follow-up; human edits and sends from Inbox.

How this helps: The SDR spends less time staring at a blank screen. ARIA drafts replies and suggests who to contact next; NEXARA keeps inbox and campaigns in one workspace.

8. Analyst

Who they are: Explains performance and builds reports. Read-only on escalations.

Example workflow: Explain performance and build report

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Opens NEXARA; goes to Dashboard or Analytics.Sidebar: Ops, Research, Revenue. Escalations in read-only (view only, no Approve).ARIA says: "This week's data is ready. CPL improved 18% — want the breakdown?" Quick actions: "Explain what's driving performance", "Generate weekly report", "Find the top channel".
2Clicks "Explain what's driving performance".—ARIA answers with main drivers (e.g. Ad Set 1 scaling, subject-line test, fewer low-intent leads); offers to add to weekly report.
3Asks: "Generate weekly report."—ARIA produces a one-page report: metrics, comparison to prior week, top drivers, one risk.

How this helps: The analyst gets ARIA to do the first pass on "why" and "so what." NEXARA gives one place for data and reports; they add judgment and present.

9. Client (external)

Who they are: Agency's client. Sees a simplified view: their results and content.

Example workflow: Check results and next steps

StepHumanNEXARAARIA
1Logs into client portal (Agency plan).Simple layout: Dashboard, Content, Inbox, Settings. No internal ops or team.ARIA says: "Hi! Your campaign is on track — 312 leads so far this month." Quick actions: "How are my results?", "When will I hit my goal?", "What happens next week?".
2Clicks "How are my results?"—ARIA answers in plain language: leads vs target, best channel, what's in approval.
3Asks: "When will I hit my goal?"—ARIA: at current pace, projection; options to boost if they want to exceed goal.

How this helps: The client gets answers without waiting for an email. NEXARA gives them one place to see results and content; ARIA speaks in simple, campaign-level language.
